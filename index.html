Your belly button has its own tiny ecosystem. A 2012 research review showed that your average belly button may be home to 67 bacteria types. Fungi and other germs can also get trapped inside your belly button region.

The most common skin bacteria encountered on participants in the Belly Button Biodiversity Project belonged to the groups: Staphylococci: (family of bacteria that causes staph infections), Corynebacteria: (family of bacteria that causes diphtheria), Actinobacteria: (family of bacteria that causes vaginal inflammation), Clostridiales: (family of bacteria that causes botulism and tetanus - mostly in animals), and Bacilli: (family of bacteria that causes ear infections, meningitis & anthrax).
